# PRIMUS — Campaign Bible

*A Westmarch hexcrawl toward the Dreaming Forest, played on Foundry VTT via the Cavril module suite.*

This is the writers'-room skeleton. It does not replace the lore already encoded in
`cavril-wayfarer.js` (`JOURNEY_THREADS`, `BIOME_THEMES`, `FEATURE_THEMES`, `EVENT_SEEDS`,
`MerchantEconomy`) — it **extends and interweaves it**. Every name below either comes from
those threads or is a new face attached to one of them. When in doubt, the in-code thread text
is canon; this document is the connective tissue between the threads.

---

## Tone & Principles

- **The road goes one way: toward the forest.** Every biome is a leg of a single pilgrimage that
  half the world is making and no one fully chose. The party are not the only travellers; they are
  the ones who notice.
- **The fey always take.** That is the world's first law (Wrenna's law). Bargains are real,
  binding, and asymmetric. A gift accepted is a claim. *Grudging payment costs double.* Generosity
  is the only safe currency, and it is never quite enough.
- **Grounded to epic, in the spirit of Heliana's Guide.** Most beats are mundane — a cold trapline,
  a sour well, a drover with gossip — and they DROP HOOKS. The epic is earned by accumulation: the
  butterfly that casts no shadow in temperate becomes the forest opening its eyes in the deep wood.
- **Folk-horror logic.** Threats have rules, customs, and prices, not just stat blocks. The
  fog-walker can be honoured. The Collector's terms can be argued. Knowing the rule is half the win.
- **Trade is texture and foreshadowing.** Roadside merchants (see `MerchantEconomy.TYPES`) are not
  vending machines — each carries one rumour and one quest hook that ties to an arc. A parts-buyer
  paying for "luminous ichor" is the Hunt, two biomes early.
- **Player choice bends payoffs, never erases them.** Most arcs end in a *bargain offered*, not a
  fight won. Refuse, honour, exploit, or co-opt — the country remembers which, and the next biome
  reflects it.
- **Recurrence over scale.** Wrenna, Sir Cadoc Vane, the Quiet Hand's glove, the Collector's ledger,
  the same drowned bell — the world feels continuous because faces and objects return changed, not
  because the numbers go up.

**Existing threads this bible builds on** (do not contradict): the Pilgrim (Wrenna), the Fading,
the Ferryman's Debt, the Hunt, the Two Sisters, the Forest Remembers, the Gilded Company (Sir Cadoc
Vane), the Quiet Hand, the Sickness Ahead, the War's Edge, the Red Star, the Collector, the
Thirst-King's Road, What Walks in the Fog, the Cooling Forge, the Scavenger-Prophet, the Spreading,
Something Mines Below, the Town That Drowned (Threnmouth), the Great Hunt, the Green That Remembers,
the Wolf-Winter, the King of the Road, the Weir-War, and the One Who Follows.

## 1. MAIN SIDE-QUEST ARCS

Six arcs, each spanning several biomes, each braided into the existing journey threads and into
each other. They are **meta-arcs**: each gathers two or more of the in-code threads under one
escalating question, gives them shared NPCs and a shared object, and lets the party's choices in one
leg bleed into the next. Run them concurrently with the in-code threads; these are the spine the
threads hang on.

---

### ARC A — "The Tithe of the Forest" (the spine)

**PREMISE.** The Dreaming Forest is not waking; it is *collecting*. Every thread the party walks —
the plague's shared dream, the river's drowned bell, the changeling Wrenna chases — is the forest
calling in a debt the living world has owed since it last bargained for spring. The party are walking
the collection route, and the forest has been told they are coming.

**BIOMES & WHY.** *Temperate* (the Fading begins — butterflies with no shadow, the hummed tune);
*water/river* (the Ferryman's Debt, the drowned bell counts the party); *boreal/jungle* (the road
ordering itself into rows as the boundary nears); *void* (the threshold, where the dead feel "one
thin step to the left"). It touches every biome lightly because the Tithe is the reason any of the
other arcs resolve toward the forest at all.

**KEY NPCs.** **Wrenna** (the Pilgrim) · the **Ferryman / his twin** · the **Green-Eyed Sister** and
the **River-Grey Sister** (the Two Sisters) · the **Courteous Guide** who steps from a solid trunk ·
the **Tithe-Warden** (new — see Cast), the fey assessor in funeral courtesy who "confirms the route"
the way the Collector's clerk does, and may BE the same office wearing a fey face.

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *Temperate* — A butterfly the colour of stained glass casts no shadow; that night the party dreams
   the same dream a plague-village will describe two legs on. The Tithe has marked them as carriers.
2. *River* — The drowned bell tolls and the current tugs the party a hand's-width toward it. The
   Ferryman names it: "The river remembers the bell, and the bell remembers a debt." The party are
   now ON the ledger.
3. *Boreal/jungle* — The Tithe-Warden falls into step (mirroring the Collector's clerk), consults a
   ledger bound in bark, and says only: "Confirming the route. You carry more than you packed." It
   counts the party — and counts one extra (the One Who Follows).
4. *Any* — A convergence beat: whichever arc the party advanced most (Hunt, Sisters, Pilgrim) sends
   its NPC to warn them what the forest will ask. Wrenna's line lands here: *give gladly, or pay double.*
5. *Void / forest threshold* — The pale ring of mushrooms, the arch of living fallen branch. The
   Tithe is named in full: the forest wants back what was taken to make the first spring, and the
   party's accumulated debts (river, sisters, hunt, plague) ARE the down payment.
6. **Payoff.** The forest opens. What the party *gladly gave* across the journey buys passage and a
   say in the bargain; what they *grudged or refused* is taken anyway, doubled, from someone they
   care about. Wrenna's fate (returns with the girl / hangs as a swaying shoe) is the barometer.

**INTERCONNECTS.** This is the arc all the others feed: the Plague (Arc D) is the Tithe leaking
upstream; the Hunt's wounded beast is "going home" to pay its own tithe; the Sisters' quarrel is two
fey collectors fighting over the party's debt; the Quiet Hand (Arc B) is trying to buy the party OUT
of the Tithe for its own ends; the Gilded Company (Arc C) thinks the Tithe is a treasure to be looted.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** Generosity banked across the campaign is literally tallied (see the Tithe-Warden's
ledger). A party that gave gladly walks out, like Wrenna-and-the-girl, "neither aged a day." A party
that hoarded loses the thing they value most to the doubled price — and the campaign's epilogue is the
forest's edge hung with their tokens, swaying in no wind.

### ARC B — "The Quiet Hand and the Open Palm"

**PREMISE.** The benefactor smoothing the party's road (the Quiet Hand) and the fey reaching for them
are the same impulse wearing two gloves. The Quiet Hand is a mortal broker — the **Glovewright** —
who has spent a fortune easing the party toward the forest because she intends to use them to settle
HER bargain with the fey, made a generation ago, that she can no longer pay alone.

**BIOMES & WHY.** *Temperate/road* (the first cache, the mended bridge, the King of the Road's toll
that the Hand quietly pays for the party); *desert* (the Hand's coin buys water from the Thirst-King's
emissary on the party's behalf — a debt of memory incurred in their name without consent); *tainted*
(the Hand's old wards ring the Spreading — she's been holding a line for years); *forest threshold*
(she reveals the one thing she'll ask).

**KEY NPCs.** The **Glovewright** (new — the Quiet Hand's face) · her runner children (the unpaid
child who hands you notes) · **Sir Cadoc Vane** of the Gilded Company, who is ALSO in the Hand's debt
and resents it · the **Collector's clerk**, who holds the Glovewright's own overdue note.

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *Road* — The first cache and the line "A friend goes ahead of you." A runner child wears a glove a
   size too big — the Glovewright's mark.
2. *Road/desert* — The Hand pays the King of the Road's tenth for the party (Feature arc crossover),
   and separately buys them Thirst-King water — sweetening the road while quietly mortgaging their
   memory. The party benefit; the cost is deferred and theirs.
3. *Any* — The colder note: "I have spent much on you. Soon I will ask one thing, and you will want to
   say yes." The glove in the wax-seal is one the party has seen on a corpse, or a king, or a clerk.
4. *Tainted* — The party find the Glovewright in person holding a failing ward at the Spreading's edge
   (Arc D / regional Spreading). She is older than her notes sounded and visibly paying for something.
5. *Forest threshold* — She asks: carry HER debt across the boundary and offer the fey a substitute —
   the party, or someone they love, in place of the child she once promised and never delivered.
6. **Payoff.** Pay her back for the smoothed road and you shoulder a fey claim that lands in Arc A's
   bargain. Refuse, and learn "what a smoothed road becomes when it turns against you" — every prior
   kindness reverses (the mended bridge fails behind you, the turned-aside patrol turns back).

**INTERCONNECTS.** The Glovewright IS the Quiet Hand thread, given a face and a motive that plugs
into the Tithe (Arc A). Her glove is the bible's recurring OBJECT — it appears on the Collector
(Arc E), hinting the two brokers trade in the same debts. Sir Cadoc (Arc C) can be turned against her
or rallied to her. Her wards at the Spreading tie her to Arc D.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** Honour the debt → the party walk into the forest already owing, raising Arc A's
stakes but earning the Glovewright as a powerful, grateful ally on the far side. Expose her to those
she manipulated (Cadoc, the villages whose patrols she diverted into danger) → she falls, her
protections lapse, and the Spreading and the King of the Road both worsen. Kill her → the unpaid fey
debt transfers to her nearest creditor: the party.

### ARC C — "Glory and the Gilded Company"

**PREMISE.** Sir Cadoc Vane's Gilded Company races the party for every bounty, treating the whole
pilgrimage as a trophy hunt — until they chase something that chases back. Cadoc learns, the hard and
expensive way, that the forest is not a dungeon to be looted, and must choose between the legend he
sells and the survival of his thinning band.

**BIOMES & WHY.** *Temperate/road* (the overtaking, the bought bounty-board); *savanna* (both bands
drawn to the Great Hunt's wrong-beast — a trophy worth a name); *volcanic* (Cadoc tries to loot the
Cooling Forge's god-hoard and it goes badly); *boreal* (his thinned company at the Wolf-Winter's
wall). Cadoc shadows the party through any biome with a "prize."

**KEY NPCs.** **Sir Cadoc Vane** (existing) · **Quartermaster Ilse Vane**, his sister and the
company's real brain (new) · the **Bone-Singer** of the savanna who sells "courage" and weighs Cadoc's
ambition · the **last priest of the Cooling Forge**, half-stone · the **Glovewright** (Cadoc owes her).

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *Temperate/road* — Cadoc overtakes the party, smirking; the bounty-board is pre-claimed "the Gilded
   Company." A local says they pay for first read.
2. *Savanna* — Both bands answer the Great Hunt. Cadoc wants the wrong-beast's head and the name it
   carries; the party can let him have the kill and the cost, or take it themselves.
3. *Volcanic* — The struck-haste camp, the dropped sword, the panic-carved name: Cadoc looted the
   Cooling Forge and woke what guards the god-hoard. Whatever he chased, chased back.
4. *Boreal* — Cadoc alone, company thinned, smirk gone, at the Wolf-Winter's wall: "There's a thing up
   ahead that doesn't care how fine your gear is. I'd take help. I never thought I'd say it."
5. *Boreal/threshold* — Ilse, more clear-eyed than her brother, offers the party the Company's maps and
   muscle for the forest — IF they help her get Cadoc out alive instead of letting his pride finish them.
6. **Payoff.** Make an ally, a debt, or a corpse of Cadoc. An allied Company is a war-band at the
   forest's edge (huge in Arc A's bargain-by-force ending). A dead or shamed Cadoc leaves Ilse either
   a grateful new ally or a vengeful enemy who blames the party for not saving him.

**INTERCONNECTS.** Cadoc owes the Glovewright (Arc B) and can be the lever that exposes or saves her.
His looting of the Cooling Forge is the volcanic regional arc's inciting "someone got here first." His
trophy-greed makes him the perfect foil to Arc A's *give gladly* thesis: the Company TAKES, and the
forest is the one place taking is fatal.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** The Company is the campaign's variable army. Their banners at the threshold change
whether the party can *demand* a bargain or must *beg* one. Cadoc redeemed becomes a tragic-heroic
sacrifice option in the finale; Cadoc spurned defects to whoever opposes the party (the Scavenger-
Prophet, the Red Star's flock, even the fey).

### ARC D — "The Shared Dream" (the plague that isn't)

**PREMISE.** A sickness runs ahead of the party up every road, and its victims all dream the same
dream of the same far place — the Dreaming Forest. It is not a plague; it is the Tithe leaking
backward through the water, the forest's hunger reaching downstream. To cure it the party must travel
TOWARD its source — the forest — which is also where it gets worse.

**BIOMES & WHY.** *Temperate/river* (the white-crossed doors, "don't drink downstream of us"; it moves
on water); *tainted* (the Spreading — the same wrongness, geographic now, growing toward a seed);
*wasteland* (a downstream town already dead of it, the Scavenger-Prophet's flock immune for ominous
reasons); *frozen/tundra* (the dream freezes people smiling, breathing in time — the Fog-walker's fog
carries it). Follows watercourses across biomes.

**KEY NPCs.** **Wrenna** (her grief is the dream's purest carrier — she's been dreaming it for seven
years); the **plague-doctor pedlar** (tainted merchant, cures-and-curses); **Sister Maready**, a
field-healer racing it and losing (new); the **Apothecary** who outfits the party to carry medicine in;
the **Glovewright**, whose wards are the only thing slowing the Spreading.

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *River-village* — Shuttered doors, white crosses, "It came up the road. It is going your way too."
2. *River-inn* — A day behind the fever; the healer (Sister Maready) is out of everything. Herbalist's
   simples and an apothecary's medicines are worth more than gold here.
3. *Any* — The tell: the sick share one dream and wake describing the same far place. Wrenna, if
   present, finishes their sentences. This is not only an illness.
4. *Tainted* — The Spreading: the dream made into landscape, a line of dead grass advancing toward (or
   from) a seed. A journal: "It is not a sickness. It is a seed. And someone planted it."
5. *Wasteland/source* — The fouled well / buried relic / thing in the water. Here it can be ended,
   carried onward, or harvested. Whole downstream villages live or die by the choice.
6. **Payoff.** Close the source and the dream recedes (and Arc A's Tithe loses a tendril — the forest
   reaches one biome less far). Carry it knowingly and you become the Tithe's courier (dark Arc A
   ending). Take a cutting (the Spreading's offer) and you walk a slow promise into the forest itself.

**INTERCONNECTS.** The Shared Dream is the Tithe (Arc A) seen from downstream — same phenomenon, mortal
scale. It IS the in-code Sickness Ahead and the Spreading, braided. The Glovewright's wards (Arc B)
gate it; the Scavenger-Prophet's immune flock (regional wasteland) is a horror-reveal that ties the
plague to the prophet's waking relic. Sister Maready can become a recurring ally who reappears at the
forest's edge, cured or claimed.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** This arc is the campaign's MORAL ledger for "downstream people you'll never meet."
Curing sources banks goodwill that the Tithe-Warden (Arc A) literally counts. Letting villages fall —
or weaponising the dream — sours the country: later merchants raise prices, settlements bar gates, and
the One Who Follows (Arc F) turns out to be someone the party let die of it.

### ARC E — "The Ledger and the Red Star"

**PREMISE.** A red star burns in the evening sky and a barefoot prophet preaches the end beneath it,
gathering a flock — and a pale clerk in funeral black, the Collector, walks the same roads confirming a
debt "incurred long ago, by blood or by bargain." The star, the prophet, and the Collector are three
readings of one event: the Dreaming Forest's bargain coming due on the whole country at once, and
someone is deciding who pays.

**BIOMES & WHY.** *Temperate/road* (the star rises; the prophet preaches at a crossroads; the clerk
"confirms the route"); *desert* (the Thirst-King's emissary and the Collector trade in the same coin —
forgotten memories, paid years); *wasteland* (the Scavenger-Prophet's flock and the Red Star's flock
are the same fearful tide finding different prophets); *void* (the star at zenith; the ledger's true
nature). Ungated — it shadows the party everywhere, like the omen it is.

**KEY NPCs.** The **Collector** (the pale clerk, existing) · the **Barefoot Prophet** of the Red Star ·
the **Scavenger-Prophet** of the wasteland (a mirror — salvage-faith vs. star-faith) · the
**Thirst-King's veiled emissary** (memory-broker) · those the Collector has visited: "a man who gave up
his name, a woman who pays in years."

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *Road* — The red star rises; no two people read it alike. The Collector falls into step: "Not here
   for you. Not yet. Only confirming the route."
2. *Crossroads* — The Barefoot Prophet preaches beneath the star; in the crowd, faces the party have
   seen before, a little closer to him each town. Fear is curdling toward a target.
3. *Desert/wasteland* — The party meet others the Collector has visited and learn the terms can be
   ARGUED. The Thirst-King's emissary and the Collector are revealed to deal in the same currency —
   memory, years, names — hinting the fey and the ledger are one market.
4. *Wasteland* — The Scavenger-Prophet's relic wakes (regional arc); the Red Star's flock and the
   salvage-flock bleed together into one panicked mass looking for someone to blame or to follow.
5. *Void* — Beneath the star at zenith, the prophet's flock gathers; the Collector lays the ledger
   open and names the price. The ledger's pages, the party see, are bark — the same as the Tithe-
   Warden's (Arc A). The Collector is the forest's accountant in the mortal world.
6. **Payoff.** Defuse, expose, join, or scatter the prophet's flock; pay, contest, or refuse the
   Collector's price. The star "sets on a changed country either way" — and how the party leave it
   (panicked / unified / leaderless) decides who marches with them, or against them, to the forest.

**INTERCONNECTS.** The Collector and the Tithe-Warden (Arc A) share a ledger of bark — strongly imply
they are one entity, mortal-faced and fey-faced. The Glovewright (Arc B) holds an overdue note with the
Collector, so exposing one threatens the other. The Red Star is the SKY-LEVEL omen of Arc A's Tithe;
the prophet's flock is the human stampede the forest's hunger creates. Joining the prophet is a way to
arrive at the forest with a mob — a darker version of Arc C's allied army.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** Argue the Collector's terms cleverly and the party can shave their Tithe debt
(Arc A) — the one place mortal wit beats the fey. Refuse outright and "learn what interest the ledger
charges on a broken word" (a recurring debuff/shadow that escalates to the One Who Follows). Expose the
prophet as a fear-merchant and the country calms; let him crown the star and the finale opens on a
country already half-given to the forest before the party arrive.

### ARC F — "The One Who Follows"

**PREMISE.** Someone walks behind the party — boot-prints their number plus one, small gifts left
where they'll be found, a figure on the dusk ridge raising one hand. It is not a threat; it is a
return. The follower is a debt, a rival, or a grief the party themselves created earlier on the road,
shaped by whatever they did or failed to do — and it has come to be there at the forest, where all
debts are paid.

**BIOMES & WHY.** Ungated — it appears in *every* biome, always at the edge: behind in soft ground, on
the far ridge, banking the fire, at the treeline. It thickens as the party near the *void/forest*
threshold, where the follower finally steps into the firelight.

**KEY NPCs.** Whoever the party left a debt with — selected by the GM from this campaign's actual
events. Strong default candidates: **a villager the party let die of the Shared Dream** (Arc D); **Sir
Cadoc Vane** if spurned (Arc C); **Sister Maready** if claimed by the forest; **Wrenna's withered
changeling**, grown; **the Glovewright's runner child**, paid at last in the only coin left. The
follower is whichever thread the party treated most carelessly.

**BEAT SKELETON.**
1. *Any* — Boot-prints behind: the party's number, plus one, learning to walk inside theirs.
2. *Any* — Things go missing and appear: a trinket gone, the fire banked, a single fresh flower laid
   where they'll find it. (Cross-reference: the Quiet Hand's kindnesses, but wrong, intimate.)
3. *Any* — The dusk-ridge figure raises one hand — greeting, not threat — nearer each evening.
4. *Void/threshold* — The follower steps into the firelight and is someone tied to a road already
   walked. What they came to say bends the finale: a debt to pay, a warning to heed, a forgiveness to
   accept or refuse.
5. **Payoff.** The follower is the campaign's mirror. If the party gave gladly (Arc A's thesis), the
   follower is a friend come to stand with them at the threshold — Wrenna, returned; Cadoc, redeemed;
   Maready, cured. If they took and grudged, it is the person they wronged, come to be paid in front
   of the fey, and the forest sides with the creditor.

**INTERCONNECTS.** Arc F is the in-code "One Who Follows" thread, made into the campaign's verdict:
it RESOLVES INTO whichever other arc the party handled worst. It is the personal face of Arc A's Tithe,
of Arc E's ledger, and of Arc D's downstream dead. The follower's identity is not fixed in this bible
on purpose — the writers' room and GM seat it in the party's real history.

**CHOICE BENDS IT.** This is the arc where choice is *everything* — the follower is generated by the
campaign, not by the bible. Its only fixed rule: it arrives, and it is owed something, and it gets
paid at the forest. The party decide, by how they travelled, whether that payment is a reunion or a reckoning.

  world died of wanting. We take only what the dead no longer need" — and one relic is waking under him.*

### Local Anchors (faces of the regional arcs)

- **The Reindeer-Herder** · Human herder · *the Herd-folk* · regional tundra (Fog-walker), Arc D ·
  WANTS to keep his fire and never lose two from inside it. · *Shares his fire on one condition: never
  step beyond it in the fog. "It takes one. Always one."*
- **The Last Priest of the Forge** · Human, half-stone · *the smith-god's order* · regional volcanic
  (Cooling Forge), Arc C · WANTS the eternal flame fed before the smith-god forges nothing. · *Half

- **The Bone-Singer** · Human ritualist · *the Hunt-camp* · regional savanna (Great Hunt), Arc C ·
  WANTS the trophies of great beasts, to sell back their courage. · *Buys what you killed and sells you
  its nerve; weighs Cadoc's ambition with a singer's cold ear.*
- **The Weir-Keeper** · Human, weary · *neither river-town* · Feature arc (Weir-War); river · WANTS the
  feud over and the hidden profiteer exposed. · *Slips you the truth both towns won't hear: a third
  party has grown fat on this war for years.*
- **The Plague-Doctor Pedlar** · Human (masked) merchant · *himself* · Arc D, regional tainted; tainted,

- **The One Who Follows** · *campaign-generated* · *the party's own past* · Arc F; every biome's edge ·

## 3. PER-BIOME ENCOUNTER TAXONOMY

Target counts per category for the writers' room, per biome. Categories: **Combat** (uses the biome's
`BIOME_BANDS` roster) · **Social/parley** · **Discovery/site** (extends `BIOME_THEMES[biome].site`) ·
**Hazard/environmental** · **Puzzle/skill-challenge**. Totals are deliberately uneven — each biome
leans toward what makes it distinctive. One line per biome on its signature.

**Distinctive signatures:**

- **temperate** — Bluebells over hoof-torn earth; worked land with no one in sight. The starter biome,
  where the Fading first whispers (the shadowless butterfly). People are plentiful and talkative
  (highest social tie with savanna/desert/water) — bandits, druids, and the first dropped hooks. Foes:
  Wolf/Boar/Bandit up through Treant and Adult Green Dragon.
- **boreal** — Green flat light under spruce, sound gone dead; warm den-mouths in frost-rimed deadfall.
  The Wolf-Winter's pack runs through here. Sprung traplines and warm-stewed empty cabins make
  discovery uneasy. Foes lean beast/giant/fey: Worg, Werebear, Frost Giant, Treant.
- **jungle** — Everything breathes; the canopy drips with no rain. The Green That Remembers feeds a god
  here. Highest discovery + hazard mix (stepped temples, sinkhole pools, things that are almost words).
  Foes: dinosaurs and serpents — Velociraptor, Yuan-ti, Giant Ape, Tyrannosaurus.
- **desert** — Heat-shimmer cities that fall; bones the wind arranges into near-circles. The Thirst-King
  trades memory for water — so SOCIAL and PUZZLE run high (parley with the Emissary, riddle-marked
  doors). Thirst itself is the great hazard. Foes: Jackal/Lamia/Mummy up to Androsphinx, Efreeti, Marid.
- **savanna** — Grass to the horizon moving in a wind you can't feel; predators fat and unbothered. The
  Great Hunt gathers many peoples under truce — so combat AND social both peak. Foes: Lion, Gnoll Pack
  Lord, Elephant, Triceratops, Tyrannosaurus.
- **frozen** — Cold with a glassy creak; aurora that gives your shadow three edges. Hazard-dominant
  (crevasses, blue-ice bodies mid-fall, exposure). The dream freezes people smiling here. Foes: Polar
  Bear, Winter Wolf, Abominable Yeti, Adult White Dragon.
- **tundra** — Flat white where distance lies; fog that moves the ground beneath it. What Walks in the
  Fog hunts here — so social (the Herd-folk's terrible hospitality) and puzzle (its counting-rules) run
  high, combat low. The fog IS the hazard. Foes: Worg, Mammoth, Saber-Toothed Tiger, Frost Giant.
- **volcanic** — Ground that ticks with heat, air like struck flint; steam that screams words. The
  Cooling Forge's anvil rings somewhere ahead. Hazard + puzzle dominant (lava, vent-heat, the forge's
  bargain-as-mechanism). Foes: Magmin, Salamander, Fire Giant, Adult Red Dragon, Pit Fiend.
- **wasteland** — Nothing grows; wind moves dust through dead ribs. The Scavenger-Prophet's scrap-town
  almost works. High discovery (bunkered ruins, picked-over hulks) and undead-heavy combat. Foes:
  Zombie/Skeleton/Wight up to Lich and Death Knight.
- **tainted** — Light a degree too red, your shadow a beat slow; flies that move in watching patterns.
  The Spreading grows toward a seed. Highest total — combat, discovery, and puzzle all heavy (defiled
  chapels, broken-outward wards). Foes: aberration/fiend/undead — Vampire, Beholder, Lich, Death Knight.
- **void** — Stars in the wrong places, one getting closer; gravity that hesitates. The forest's
  threshold. Puzzle-dominant (the highest in the bible) — distance and direction come loose; reality is
  the obstacle. Foes: Shadow, Specter, Invisible Stalker, Mind Flayer, Beholder, Lich.
- **water** — Salt over the river's smell; a bell offshore with no boat to swing it. Threnmouth drowned
  here. Balanced and high-social (ferrymen, smugglers, merfolk) with tide as the recurring hazard. Foes:
  Reef Shark, Sahuagin, Water Elemental, Giant Shark, Kraken, Adult Bronze Dragon.

> **Note for the room:** river/road/forest/mountain/hill/coast are FEATURES, not biomes — they overlay
> any biome (see `FEATURE_THEMES` + `FEATURE_ROSTER`). Budget a few extra encounters per feature where it
> recurs (the river toward the forest leans faintly fey; roads carry waylayers and the King's toll).

## 4. CROSS-BIOME CONNECTIVITY (10 threads)

Specific links that make the 12 biomes read as one continuous country, not twelve set-pieces. Each
names the biomes, NPCs, arcs, or objects involved.

1. **The river carries the dream downstream.** The Shared Dream (Arc D) moves on water: a fouled source
   in *wasteland/tainted* sickens *temperate* river-villages days downstream ("don't drink downstream of
   us"). The same current carries the Ferryman's drowned bell (Arc A) and Threnmouth's tolling
   (regional water). One watercourse, three biomes, three arcs — trace it upstream and it ends at the forest.

2. **The Glovewright operates in three biomes at once.** The Quiet Hand (Arc B) mends a bridge in
   *temperate*, buys Thirst-King water in *desert*, and holds a failing ward at the Spreading in
   *tainted* — the party feel one benefactor's reach across a third of the map before they ever see her face.

3. **The Collector and the Tithe-Warden share a bark-bound ledger.** The pale clerk (Arc E, *road/desert/
   void*) and the fey assessor (Arc A, *boreal/jungle/void*) carry the same impossible ledger. Meeting both
   is the campaign's quiet reveal that the forest's hunger and the mortal world's debts are one account.

4. **Sir Cadoc Vane shadows every prize.** The Gilded Company (Arc C) appears wherever there's a trophy:
   the *savanna* Great Hunt's wrong-beast, the *volcanic* Cooling Forge's god-hoard, the *boreal* Wolf-
   Winter's wall. He is the recurring rival who always got there first — until the once he wishes he hadn't.

5. **The fey-silver arrow points the way.** The Huntsman's arrow (Arc A, the Hunt) recovered in *river*
   country still points "home" — toward the *forest* threshold. Carried, it's a compass to the spine arc;
   the luminous ichor and humming antler-tine harvested from the same beast are sold to the parts-buyer
   merchant two biomes early, foreshadowing the kill.

6. **The Red Star burns over all twelve biomes.** The omen (Arc E) hangs in every evening sky; the
   Barefoot Prophet's flock swells town to town across *temperate/road*, and the same fear curdles into
   the Scavenger-Prophet's salvage-faith in *wasteland*. One star, two prophets, a whole country looking
   for someone to follow or to blame.

7. **The King of the Road ties the settlements together — and the Glovewright pays his toll.** The toll-
   king (Feature arc) holds every *road* across every biome; refuse him and milestones loop forever. The
   Quiet Hand (Arc B) quietly pays the party's tenth, so the road's continuity and a benefactor's reach are
   the same thread felt twice.

8. **The two prophets are one fearful tide.** The Red Star flock (*temperate/void*) and the Heirs of the
   scrap-town (*wasteland*) are the same human stampede the forest's hunger creates, finding different
   prophets (Arc E + regional wasteland). Resolve one and the other shifts; let both swell and the finale
   opens on a country already half-given.

9. **The fog and the cold carry the dream north.** What Walks in the Fog (*tundra*) and the smiling
   frozen sleepers (*frozen*) are the Shared Dream (Arc D) in winter form — the fog counts the taken, the
   ice keeps them breathing in time. The Herd-folk's "owe a death" custom is the Tithe (Arc A) in a
   colder dialect.

10. **The One Who Follows walks every edge.** The follower (Arc F) appears at the treeline in *every*
    biome — behind in soft ground, on the dusk ridge, banking the fire — thickening toward the *void/
    forest* threshold. Whoever they turn out to be is drawn from the party's actual crossings of arcs
    A–E, so the world's most continuous thread is the one the party wrote themselves.
