# PRIMUS — Recurring NPC Cast (Campaign-Codex Profiles)

*Companion to `PRIMUS-BIBLE.md`. Expands the 24-NPC recurring cast (Bible §2) into
Campaign-Codex-ready journals that also drive the procedural NPC generator.*

**Canon precedence:** in-code `JOURNEY_THREADS` text > `PRIMUS-BIBLE.md` > these profiles.
Thread names, factions, and the recurring objects (the bark-bound ledger, the Glovewright's
glove, the child's shoe, the fey-silver arrow, the drowned bell) are reused exactly.

**OCEAN key (for the generator):** each NPC carries five traits scaled **0–3** —
**O**penness, **C**onscientiousness, **E**xtroversion, **A**greeableness, **N**euroticism.
These match against CityHUD citizens; the numbers are tuned to the character, not averaged.

## Wrenna

**Name** · Wrenna *(the Pilgrim; "the one who asks")*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Human · female · looks ~40, but has walked this road before and "remembers the price" — older than she looks
**Faction / allegiance** · Unaligned (the Forest's, eventually) — a Pilgrim
**Role** · Wildcard / ally (and Arc A's living barometer)
**OCEAN** · O **3** · C **1** · E **0** · A **2** · N **3**
**Appearance** · A small woman gone weather-grey, a child's single shoe knotted to a cord at her throat. Her feet are bare and river-pale, callused to leather. She looks at the treeline the way other people look at a door left open.
**Voice & mannerism** · Voice low, unhurried, finishing other people's sentences when the dream is in them. · Never asks your name — and flinches, very slightly, if you offer it.
**Want** · Her stolen daughter back, and to be "the one who asks" — to make the bargain herself this time.
**Secret** · She has walked to the forest before. Last time she gave grudgingly and lost; she knows *give gladly, or pay double* not as wisdom but as a scar, and she half-suspects the girl who calls her "sister" now is not the daughter she lost.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Pilgrim / the Tithe), Arc D (the Shared Dream — her grief is its purest carrier), Arc F (the One Who Follows) · temperate → void
**Bio** · Seven years ago the fog took Wrenna's daughter from inside a circle of firelight, and Wrenna has been walking toward the Dreaming Forest ever since — not the first time, the country whispers, and not the first daughter. She has dreamed the Shared Dream (Arc D) longer than any plague-village, which is why the fevered finish their sentences when she passes, and why **Sister Maready** marks her presence as the surest sign the sickness "is not only an illness." Wrenna's law is the world's first law: *the fey always take; give gladly or pay double.* She offers it freely and watches who listens. Near the **Two Sisters** she will warn you which gift to honour; near the **Tithe-Warden** she goes very still, because she knows what his bark ledger counts. The shoe at her throat is her down-payment and her proof of debt. At the void threshold her fate is Arc A's barometer: she walks out with the girl, "neither aged a day," or she is left swaying in no wind — and if the party grudged their own road, the figure on the dusk ridge (Arc F) may wear her face.

## Sister Maready

**Name** · Sister Maready *(new)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Human · female · early 30s, prematurely worn
**Faction / allegiance** · The Open Order (mendicant healers)
**Role** · Ally / quest-giver
**OCEAN** · O **2** · C **3** · E **2** · A **3** · N **2**
**Appearance** · Grey habit stiff with old salt and other people's fevers, sleeves permanently pushed past the elbow. Hands chapped raw from lye and river-water. She has the bright, over-awake eyes of someone who has stopped sleeping properly and started dreaming the same dream as her patients.
**Voice & mannerism** · Brisk, triaging, kind in the clipped way of the chronically overworked. · Counts on her fingers as she talks — doses, days, the dead.
**Want** · To outrun the Shared Dream and save *one more village* before it reaches the next.
**Secret** · The fever's victims describe a forest, and three nights ago she began to dream it too. She hasn't told her Order. She is afraid she is already carrying it forward in her own water-skins.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc D (the Shared Dream / the Sickness Ahead) · river, tainted, wasteland
**Bio** · The Open Order sends its healers out empty-handed and trusting to charity, and Sister Maready has spent her vocation a single day behind the fever, arriving at white-crossed doors with nothing left in her bag. She is the human face of Arc D: out of simples, out of sleep, and lately out of certainty that this is a sickness at all. She will pay a fortune in gratitude for an **Apothecary's** medicines or a herbalist's stock, and she knows the **Plague-Doctor Pedlar** by reputation — she will warn the party that his cures come stapled to curses. Where the line of dead grass marches (the Spreading), she has seen the **Glovewright's** failing wards and wondered who pays to hold them. If the party cure a source, Maready reappears downstream alive and grateful, an ally at the forest's edge; if they let her village fall, she is a strong candidate for the One Who Follows (Arc F) — claimed by the forest she had only begun to dream of, come to the threshold to ask why no one came in time.

## The Huntsman

**Name** · The Huntsman
**Race · Gender · Age** · Human · male · 40s, fever-thinned
**Faction / allegiance** · Unaligned
**Role** · Ally / wildcard (a walking compass to Arc A)
**OCEAN** · O **1** · C **2** · E **0** · A **1** · N **2**
**Appearance** · Lean, wind-burned, a hunter's economy in every motion gone wrong now — he sweats in cold air. A bandaged forearm seeps a thin silver-bright fluid that smells faintly of cut hazel. He carries one arrow he will not loose: fey-silver, and it always lies pointing the same way.
**Voice & mannerism** · Spare, declarative, a tracker's habit of saying only what the sign says. · Keeps checking the arrow the way other men check a wound.
**Want** · To follow the beast he shot "home, one road or the other" — to finish it or be finished.
**Secret** · He knows the wound won't close because the beast was going *home to pay its own tithe*, and his arrow is now part of that debt. Following it is not a hunt anymore; it's a summons he can't refuse.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Hunt) · river → forest
**Bio** · He shot something in the deep country that bled luminous ichor and hummed from a severed antler-tine, and nothing has been right since. The fey-silver arrow he recovered in river country still points "home" — toward the forest threshold — making him a living compass for the spine arc (Arc A). Cross-country, the **roadside parts-buyers** (see the Eel-Wife and her kind) pay strange prices for "luminous ichor" and "a humming tine," foreshadowing his kill two biomes before the party reach it. The **Tithe-Warden** will count the Huntsman as already half-owed; the wound is the down-payment the forest took at the moment of the shot. He travels alone, distrusts company, and will fall in with the party only because they are walking his direction. If the beast was "going home to pay," then so is he — and the question Arc A asks of him is whether the forest accepts the arrow back, or keeps the man who carried it.

## The Green-Eyed Sister

**Name** · The Green-Eyed Sister *(one of the Two Sisters)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Fey · presents female · ageless (appears a young woman)
**Faction / allegiance** · The Dreaming Forest
**Role** · Wildcard / quest-giver (the gift that binds well, if honoured)
**OCEAN** · O **3** · C **2** · E **2** · A **3** · N **0**
**Appearance** · Eyes the green of light through canopy, too steady to be wholly human. Bare feet that leave no print in soft ground. She is always *just there*, and then between one step and the next she is not.
**Voice & mannerism** · Warm, courteous, with the over-precise diction of someone for whom every word is a clause. · Presses small gifts into your palm and closes your fingers over them.
**Want** · The party to honour her gift (a single hazelnut) and *refuse her sister's water*.
**Secret** · The hazelnut is a genuine kindness — and also a claim, smaller and gentler than her sister's, but a claim. She and the River-Grey Sister are not rivals for the party's love; they are two collectors quarrelling over the same debt, and the "generous" sister's terms are simply the ones that cost double later if grudged.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Two Sisters) · any
**Bio** · The Two Sisters are the bible's purest lesson in *the fey always take.* The Green-Eyed Sister presses a hazelnut into your hand "for a thirst you can't yet name," then is gone — and in the desert, parched past sense, that nut becomes water drawn from nowhere, the gladly-given gift repaying itself. She wants you to honour her and to spurn the **River-Grey Sister's** dipper, and **Wrenna** (who knows both of them of old) will tell you the green gift is the safer one. But "safer" is not "free": even her generosity is a thread the **Tithe-Warden** can tally. The sisters' quarrel, the bible says, is two fey fighting over the party's debt — accept the hazelnut *gladly* and you have chosen which collector holds your note. She appears in any biome, at the edge of the firelight or the bend of the road, and her courtesy never slips, because for the fey courtesy is the contract.

## The River-Grey Sister

**Name** · The River-Grey Sister *(one of the Two Sisters)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Fey · presents female · ageless (appears a young woman)
**Faction / allegiance** · The Dreaming Forest
**Role** · Wildcard / antagonist-tempter (the gift that takes, if grudged)
**OCEAN** · O **3** · C **1** · E **2** · A **1** · N **1**
**Appearance** · Skin the colour of river silt under cloud, hair that drips though no rain has fallen. She carries a wooden dipper that is always full and always sweating cold. Her thumb, when it touches you, leaves a mark that aches toward the forest.
**Voice & mannerism** · Coaxing, a little sad, the patience of moving water. · Offers the dipper at exactly the moment your throat is driest.
**Want** · The party to drink her water and carry the forest home in their dreams.
**Secret** · The water is sweet "past reason" because it is the same current that carries the Shared Dream (Arc D); to drink is to volunteer as the Tithe's courier. Refuse and her cold-thumb mark is the lesser claim — but it is still a claim, a tether pulling toward the void threshold.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Two Sisters), bleeds into Arc D (her water IS the dream's current) · river
**Bio** · Where her sister gives a dry hazelnut, the River-Grey Sister gives drink — and that is the trap. Her dipper is sweet past reason, and her water is the very current the **Ferryman** poles you across and the **drowned bell** tolls beneath; it is also, downstream, the medium of the Shared Dream (Arc D), so to swallow is to carry the forest in your sleep. **Wrenna** will beg you not to drink. Refuse, and the cold press of the Sister's thumb leaves a mark that "aches toward the forest" — the smaller debt, but a debt the **Tithe-Warden** can read off your skin like a line of the ledger. She is not cruel; she is water, and water always takes the low road home. The bible frames the sisters' feud as two collectors arguing over the party's debt, which means accepting *her* terms hands your note to the harsher creditor — the one whose price is paid in dreams, and then in person, at the void.

## The Tithe-Warden

**Name** · The Tithe-Warden *(new)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Fey (fae-noble assessor) · presents male · ageless, presents as a courtly grey-templed clerk
**Faction / allegiance** · The Dreaming Forest
**Role** · Antagonist / broker (the forest's auditor; Arc A's mechanism)
**OCEAN** · O **2** · C **3** · E **1** · A **1** · N **0**
**Appearance** · A tall figure in funeral courtesy — bark-brown coat, gloves, a face composed like a condolence. He carries a ledger bound in living bark that creaks when opened. When he counts your party he points a finger at each of you — and at one more than is there.
**Voice & mannerism** · Measured, gracious, terminally polite — the voice of a man delivering bad news he considers settled. · Wets a fingertip to turn the bark pages, though they are wood.
**Want** · To confirm the route and tally precisely what the party owe.
**Secret** · His ledger is the *same* ledger the **Collector** carries — same bark, same hand. He may not be a separate being at all but the forest's accountancy wearing a fey face where the Collector wears a mortal one. The "one extra" he counts is the One Who Follows (Arc F), already on the books.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Tithe — its mechanism), Arc E (shares the bark ledger with the Collector) · boreal, jungle, void
**Bio** · Where the boundary nears and the road begins ordering itself into rows, the Tithe-Warden falls into step exactly as the **Collector's** clerk does on the mortal highway, and says only: *"Confirming the route. You carry more than you packed."* He is the forest's assessor, and his bark-bound ledger is the campaign's literal tally of generosity given and grudged — every gladly-given gift across Arcs A–E is a credit here; every hoarded kindness, a doubled debit. He counts the party and counts one extra, which is the first hard proof of the One Who Follows (Arc F). Meeting both him and the Collector is the quiet reveal of Cross-Thread §3: the forest's hunger and the mortal world's debts are *one account.* The **Glovewright** dreads him, because her unpaid generation-old promise sits open in his pages; **Wrenna** knows him of old and lowers her eyes. At the void, his is the voice that names the Tithe in full and presents the bill the party have been running up since the first shadowless butterfly.

## The Courteous Guide

**Name** · The Courteous Guide
**Race · Gender · Age** · Fey (threshold-guide) · presents androgynous · ageless
**Faction / allegiance** · The Dreaming Forest
**Role** · Wildcard / guide (the door that is always open)
**OCEAN** · O **2** · C **2** · E **2** · A **2** · N **0**
**Appearance** · Steps out of a solid trunk as though through a curtain, bark closing behind without a seam. Dressed in the muted greens and greys of deep wood, a host's small bow always ready. Smiles with a host's complete, untroubled certainty.
**Voice & mannerism** · Gracious, unhurried, faintly amused that you thought there was a choice. · Gestures you onward with an open hand, palm up, like a maître d'.
**Want** · To bring the expected guests in.
**Secret** · There is no wrong path. Every route the party choose arrives at the same place; the Guide's courtesy is the velvet over the fact that the forest has already decided they will enter. "This way, or that way" is not an offer — it is a reassurance to the doomed.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Forest Remembers) · forest, void
**Bio** · *"You are expected. You are always expected. This way, or that way; they arrive at the same place."* The Courteous Guide is the forest's host, and his bottomless graciousness is the most frightening thing about him, because it never depends on what the party do. He appears at the threshold of the deep wood, stepping from a living trunk, to walk the expected guests in — and the party are always the expected guests. Where the **Tithe-Warden** audits and the **Collector** demands, the Guide simply welcomes, which is its own kind of trap: he frames inevitability as hospitality. **Wrenna** has been led by him before and follows without hesitation, which tells the party everything. If the party walked the road *giving gladly* (Arc A's thesis), his welcome is true and the forest receives them as guests; if they grudged, the same smile ushers them to a reckoning, because the door was never the choice — only the manner of the entering was.

**Secret** · She may not be Wrenna's daughter anymore, or ever again. Whatever the forest took, it has kept her seven years unaged and taught her a new kin-word; the child Wrenna chases and the lure pulling the whole party toward the void may be the same instrument wearing one small face.

In Arc F she is named explicitly as a possible One Who Follows: "Wrenna's withered changeling, grown" — the gift the forest returns when the party gave grudgingly, come to the firelight at last. Whether she is daughter, changeling, or the Tithe itself with a child's mouth is the question her mother has walked the world twice to ask.

## The Glovewright

**Name** · The Glovewright *(the Quiet Hand; "a friend goes ahead of you")*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Human · female · 60s, older than her notes ever sounded
**Faction / allegiance** · Herself — once promised to the Forest (the Quiet Hand)
**Role** · Broker / quest-giver (Arc B's engine)
**OCEAN** · O **2** · C **3** · E **1** · A **2** · N **2**
**Appearance** · A spare, upright woman who looks like a retired notary until you see her hands — always gloved, in fine kid that has gone soft with wear. Her wax seals carry the imprint of a single glove. In person, at the Spreading's edge, she is greyer and more frightened than any of her elegant notes suggested, visibly paying for something with her own years.
**Voice & mannerism** · Precise, warm, the controlled courtesy of someone who has bought a great many favours. · Touches the seam of her glove when she lies, or when the debt presses.
**Want** · The party to settle the fey debt she can no longer pay alone.
**Secret** · A generation ago she promised the forest a child and never delivered. The whole smoothed road — the caches, the mended bridge, the **King of the Road's** paid tenth, the bought Thirst-King water — is her courting the party as the *substitute* she intends to offer in that child's place: them, or someone they love.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc B (the Quiet Hand — its face and motive), Arc D (her wards gate the Spreading) · road, desert, tainted
**Bio** · She is the Quiet Hand, the benefactor who goes ahead of the party leaving caches and mending bridges, and the bible gives her a face and a debt: the Glovewright spent a fortune easing strangers toward the forest because she means to use them to settle a bargain she made a generation ago and can no longer pay. Cross-country her reach is felt three biomes at once (Cross-Thread §2): a mended bridge in temperate, **Thirst-King** water bought in the party's name in desert, a failing ward at the Spreading in tainted. Her glove is the bible's recurring object — it surfaces on the **Collector**, who holds her own overdue note, hinting the two brokers trade the same debts. **Sir Cadoc Vane** is also in her debt and resents it; he can be turned against her or rallied to her. At the threshold she asks the one thing she has been buying toward all along — carry her fey claim across, offer the forest a substitute. Pay her back and you shoulder a claim that lands in Arc A's bargain; refuse, and "a smoothed road" reverses — the mended bridge fails behind you, the diverted patrol turns back.

## The Collector

**Name** · The Collector *(the pale clerk)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Undead, or worse — clerk · presents male · indeterminate; looks 50, has looked 50 a long time
**Faction / allegiance** · The Ledger (the Forest's mortal accountancy)
**Role** · Antagonist / broker (Arc E's spine; Arc A's mortal hand)
**OCEAN** · O **1** · C **3** · E **1** · A **1** · N **0**
**Appearance** · Pale to the point of candlewax, dressed in funeral black gone slightly green at the seams. A bark-bound ledger rides under one arm, always. He wears a single glove — the Glovewright's mark — and does not seem to know, or care, that the party recognise it.
**Voice & mannerism** · Flat, courteous, utterly without urgency — a man for whom the outcome is already entered. · Pauses before your name as though checking it against a line.
**Want** · To collect a debt "by blood or by bargain" at the road ahead.
**Secret** · His ledger is the **Tithe-Warden's** ledger; he is the forest's accountant wearing a mortal face, and the pages are bark. The terms, crucially, can be *argued* — the one place mortal wit beats the fey — but he will never volunteer that.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc E (the Ledger and the Red Star), Arc A (the Tithe's mortal hand) · road, desert, void
**Bio** · *"Not here for you. Not yet. Only confirming the route."* The Collector walks the same highways as the party, a pale clerk in funeral black, confirming a debt incurred "long ago, by blood or by bargain." He is the mortal-faced twin of the **Tithe-Warden** — same impossible bark ledger (Cross-Thread §3), strongly implying the forest's hunger and the country's debts are a single account. He holds the **Glovewright's** overdue note, so exposing one broker threatens the other, and he wears her glove like a creditor wearing a debtor's ring. In the desert the party learn his coin is the **Thirst-King Emissary's** coin — memory, years, names — revealing the fey and the ledger as one market (Arc E, beat 3). His gift to the clever is that his terms can be *argued*: contest them well and the party shave their Arc A Tithe debt; refuse outright and "learn what interest the ledger charges on a broken word," a shadow that escalates into the One Who Follows. Beneath the Red Star at the void he lays the ledger open and names the price.

## The Thirst-King's Emissary

**Name** · The Thirst-King's Emissary *(the veiled herald)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Mummified herald (undead) · presents indeterminate · centuries dead
**Faction / allegiance** · The Thirst-King
**Role** · Broker / antagonist (memory-merchant)
**OCEAN** · O **1** · C **3** · E **2** · A **1** · N **0**
**Appearance** · Veiled head to foot in sun-bleached linen, the wrappings stirring even in dead air. Where skin shows it is gilded leather, lips long gone. It carries a tally-stick notched with names already taken, and a vessel of water so clear it hurts to look at.
**Voice & mannerism** · Gracious, ceremonious, dry as the wind that arranges bones into circles. · Bows over each transaction and adds a notch to the tally without being asked.
**Want** · To trade impossibly sweet water for memories "you will not miss."
**Secret** · The memories you "won't miss" are load-bearing — a name here, a face there — and the Emissary keeps *your* tally specifically. It and the **Collector** deal the same currency; the desert king and the forest's ledger are one market, and every draught is a payment toward a debt the party didn't know they were settling.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc E (memory-broker; revealed kin to the Collector's market) · desert
**Bio** · In the heat-shimmer cities that fall and rise, the Thirst-King's veiled Emissary offers the one thing the desert cannot: water sweet past sense, paid for in memories "you will not miss." Each draught costs a name, and the Emissary keeps a careful tally of yours. The bible's reveal (Arc E, beat 3; Cross-Thread §1 of trade) is that this is the **Collector's** coin exactly — memory, years, names — so the Thirst-King and the forest's ledger trade in the same market, and the fey bargain wears a desert veil here. The **Glovewright** has bought this water *in the party's name without their consent* (Arc B, beat 2), mortgaging their memory to sweeten their road — a debt deferred and theirs. The Emissary is courteous, ceremonious, and merciless in the way of all the bible's collectors: it does not cheat, it simply enforces a contract you were too thirsty to read. Refuse the water and the desert is the hazard; accept it and you have notched your own name on the tally that leads, eventually, to the same bark page everyone else's does.

## The Ferryman

**Name** · The Ferryman *(or his twin)*
**Race · Gender · Age** · Human, or his twin · male · 50s — and there appear to be two of him
**Faction / allegiance** · The River / the Debt
**Role** · Broker / quest-giver (Arc A's river beat)
**OCEAN** · O **2** · C **2** · E **1** · A **2** · N **1**
**Appearance** · A broad, slow man in oilskins that never quite dry, poling a flat boat that meets you before you have raised a hand. His face is weathered kind — and you will swear you saw the same face on the far bank as you stepped off. Coins slide off his open palm; he will not take them.
**Voice & mannerism** · Low, riverine, speaking in the river's idiom of currents and debts. · Waves off payment with the flat of his hand, every time.
**Want** · The river's favour returned, upstream, at the source.
**Secret** · There are two of him — a man and his twin, one on each bank — and the easy crossing is not free but *advanced*, like the Glovewright's smoothed road. By stepping into the boat you accept the river's gift, and "the river will want the favour returned" at the forest source where the **drowned bell** counts.
**Arcs & biomes** · Arc A (the Ferryman's Debt) · river

The **King of the Road** taxes them like everyone else, and the **Glovewright's** caches sometimes reach the party through their hands. The Eel-Wife on the water, the River-Trader at the bend, the parts-buyer where the Hunt draws near — each is biome-matched and each wants the same two things: a fair deal and a good tale, traded rumour for rumour. They are the connective gossip of a country all walking one direction, and the surest early sign that an arc is coming is a merchant who already knows its first name.
